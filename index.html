I am a Principal Statistician at Quantum Leap Healthcare Collaborative where I am a supporting statistician on the ISPY-2 main sequential, multiple assignment, randomized trail and the lead statistician on the Endocrine Optimization Platform (EOP) sub-study. I completed my Ph.D. in the Department of Statistics at North Carolina State University in December 2023. My research interests include sequential experimentation, specifically blending work from reinforcement learning and design of experiments; non-regular statistical inference from adaptively collected data; and statistical methods for dynamic treatment regimes, including design and analysis of Sequential Multiple Assignment Randomized Trials (SMARTs). I was advised by Dr. Marie Davidian and Dr. Eric Laber.

Prior to Quantum Leap, I worked as a statistician in the Semel Institute Center for Community Health at the University of California, Los Angeles. The team I worked for studies the effects of community health workers in rural communities in rural South Africa. My work involved analyzing data from cluster randomized trials conducted over the past ten years.
