I am a Ph.D. candidate in the Department of Statistics at North Carolina State University. My research interests include sequential experimentation, specifically blending work from reinforcement learning and design of experiments; non-regular statistical inference from adaptively collected data; and statistical methods for dynamic treatment regimes, including design and analysis of Sequential Multiple Assignment Randomized Trials (SMARTs). I am advised by Dr. Marie Davidian and Dr. Eric Laber.

In addition to my studies at NC State, I work as a statistician in the Semel Institute Center for Community Health at the University of California, Los Angeles. The team I work on studies the effects of community health workers in rural communities in rural South Africa. My work involves analyzing data from cluster randomized trials conducted over the past ten years.
